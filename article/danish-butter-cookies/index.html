What are Danish Butter Cookies

Danish Butter Cookies are a type of shortbread cookie known for their rich, buttery flavor and crisp texture.

Often associated with holiday tins, they are simple yet elegant cookies that melt in your mouth.

  • Buttery and Crisp - Rich in butter for a delicate, crisp bite.
  • Vanilla Flavored - A light, sweet vanilla taste balances the richness.
  • Melt-in-Your-Mouth Texture - Known for their tender, airy crumb.
  • Iconic Holidar Treat - Often found in festive tins, perfect for sharing.

Danish Butter Cookies originated in Denmark and became widely popular in the 20th century, especially through brands like Royal Dansk, which packaged them in iconic blue tins.

Once a homemade treat, they are now a holiday favorite, known for their rich flavor and delicate texture.

Key ingredients

Butter

  • Provides Richness, flavor, and a tender texture to the cookies
  • I used a salted butter for extra flavors. Unsalted butter will also work
  • Substitute Margarine or shortening can be used, but butter gives the best flavor and texture.

Granulated Sugar

  • Adds sweetness and helps with the crisp texture.
  • Substitute: Powdered sugar or coconut sugar for a different sweetness profile.

Salt

  • Balances the sweetness and enhances the overall flavor.

Egg

  • Binds the ingredients together and helps with the structure of the cookie.
  • Substitute: No egg substitute can work the same as eggs in this recipe.

Vanilla Extract

  • Pure vanilla extract adds a sweet, aromatic flavor that complements the butter.
  • Substitute: Vanilla bean paste will also work.

All-Purpose Flour

  • Provides structure and helps hold the dough together.
  • Substitute: Gluten-free flour blend or Easy Almond Flour Baking Mix for a gluten-free version.

Granulated Sugar (for topping)

  • Adds a slight crunch and extra sweetness on top of the cookies.
  • Substitute: Colored sanding sugar for a festive touch.

How to Make Danish Butter Cookies Recipe

Prep

Preheat the oven to 325°F (165°C) Fan Assist and line 2 baking sheets with parchment paper.

Make Danish Butter Cookie Dough

  1. With an electric hand mixer, cream the butter, sugar, and salt together on high speed until pale and fluffy, about 3 minutes.
  2. Add the egg and vanilla, and beat until combined.
  3. On low speed, add the flour and mix until just combined and your dough is formed with the right consistency.

Shape Danish Cookie Dough

Scoop dough into a pastry bag fitted with a large star tip (9mm) and pipe 2-inch (5cm) circles onto the prepared baking sheets. Sprinkle each biscuit with the 2 tablespoons of granulated sugar.

Bake the Cookies

Bake the trays for about 15 minutes, or until the edges are light golden brown. Let cool completely on a wire rack before storing in an airtight container.

Enjoy with a hot cup of coffee. These cookies can be stored at room temperature for up to 4 days.

Gemma's Pro Chef Tips For Making Danish Butter Cookies

  • Keep an eye on the cookies while you are baking them. They should only be a very pale golden when they are done.
  • These cookies make great gifts! Line a tin with paper cupcake liners and stack two or three cookies in each liner. It will look just like a classic tin of Danish butter cookies!
  • The primary flavors in this cookie are butter and vanilla, so you'll want to use good quality ingredients!
  • Use colored sanding sugar for a festive touch!
  • You can dip these cookies into melted milk, dark, or white chocolate.
  • Go big on homemade! Make them with homemade vanilla extract and handmade butter!

Fun Variations

  • Chocolate-dipped: After baking, dip the edges of the cookies in melted chocolate (e.g., dark, milk, or white chocolate) for an extra indulgent touch.
  • Sprinkle with festive sugars: Use colored sanding sugar or coarse sugar to add a sparkling, festive finish to your cookies, perfect for the holidays.
  • Coconut-flavored: Add shredded coconut to the dough or sprinkle it on top before baking for a tropical twist.
  • Lemon zest: Incorporate lemon zest into the dough for a refreshing citrus flavor that complements the buttery richness.
  • Almond variation: Add almond extract instead of vanilla for a nutty, aromatic version of the classic cookie.

Make Ahead and Storage Instructions

Make-Ahead

You can prepare the cookie dough up to 2 days in advance. Simply wrap it tightly in plastic wrap and refrigerate until ready to pipe and bake.

Storage

Once baked, store the Danish Butter Cookies in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 4 days.

For longer storage, place them in a sealed container and freeze for up to 2 months. Let frozen cookies thaw at room temperature for about an hour before serving.

FAQ

Why is my Danish Butter Cookies Dough Too Thick to Pipe?

If your Danish butter cookie dough is too thick or dry to pipe, here's how to fix it:

  • Add a tablespoon of milk to the dough and mix it in to soften the consistency.
  • Check the dough texture—it should be smooth, soft, and easy to pipe. If it's still too thick, add a little more milk, but avoid making it too runny.
  • Ensure proper mixing—sometimes the butter or flour can cause clumps, so make sure the dough is well-blended before adding any liquids.
  • For best results, make sure you're using quality butter, as it helps create the right texture for piping.

Why Did My Danish Cookies Spread Out and Lose Their Shape?

If your Danish butter cookies are spreading too much while baking, it could be due to a few common issues:

  • Too much butter: Excess butter can cause the cookies to spread more than desired. Use a kitchen scale to measure the butter or ensure you're using the correct ratio of ingredients.
  • Overmixing the dough: Overmixing the dough can warm up the butter and make it too soft. Mix until just combined to prevent excess spreading.
  • Warm dough: If the dough is too warm when piped or placed on the baking sheet, it will spread more while baking. Try chilling the dough for 15-30 minutes before piping and baking.
  • Incorrect oven temperature: Ensure your oven is preheated to the correct temperature, and use an oven thermometer to make sure it's accurate. Too low a temperature can cause excessive spreading.
